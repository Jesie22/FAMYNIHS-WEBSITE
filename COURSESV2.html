What is TVL Track?

The Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) track is one of four major tracks in the Philippine senior high school curriculum for the K–12 program. The TVL program is intended to prepare students for jobs, entrepreneurship, or advanced education in technical and vocational fields.

Information and Communications Technology (ICT) Strand

• Focuses on computer systems, programming, web design, and digital media.

• Prepares students for careers in IT, such as computer technicians, web developers, and network administrators.

Home Economics (HE) Strand

• Emphasizes skills in hospitality, tourism, caregiving, culinary arts, and fashion design.

• Equips students for roles in the service industry, such as chefs, bakers, and hospitality managers.

Automotive Strand

• Concentrates on automotive technology basics, including vehicle maintenance, troubleshooting, and repair.

• Prepares students for careers as automotive service technicians and mechanics.

Electrical Strand

• Covers electrical installation and maintenance, electrical systems, and wiring.

• Prepares students for careers as electricians and electrical technicians.

What is the Academic Track?

The Academic track is one of the tracks under the K-12 curriculum and also one of the four tracks in Senior High School. This track prepares students who plan to pursue college education. Famy National Integrated High School offers three strands:

Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM) Strand

• The ABM strand seeks to prepare the young business leaders of tomorrow.

• Paves the way for business-related college degrees.

• Teaches basic concepts of financial management, accounting, and corporate operations.

• Aims to instill crucial skills needed for future careers and businesses.

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) Strand

• Focuses on innovation, problem-solving, and critical thinking.

Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) Strand

• Covers fields that explore the human condition using analytical, critical, and empirical methods.

• Studies changes in society and human behavior.
